Our Challenge

There are over 4,000 individuals experiencing homeless in San Jose alone, and this problem is even larger around the Bay Area, where an estimated nearly 26,000 people are living in the Bay Area without a home. While there are many initiatives and resources developed by the County, the City, and service providers to address various components of homelessness, one common obstacle is the lack of knowledge about, access to, and coordination between critical resources.

Even where resources exist, many of these are underutilized. The question for our challenge is: how do we help those experiencing homelessness better learn about and subsequently access the services available to them?

Your Submission

Given the context of our regional housing crisis, we are seeking innnovative submissions in three broad buckets to address the access and sharing of information around essential services:

  • A mobile application/mobile-friendly web application that helps homeless individuals locate and apply to affordable housing and other social services.

    • Key Features:
      • Location-based suggestions
      • Integration with mobility apps to show public transit options
      • Up-to-date information about eligibility requirements, intake process, and services offered/available
      • Texting feature?
    • Key Resources to include:
      • Shelters and overnight warming locations
      • Affordable housing
      • Transitional housing
      • Public bathrooms
      • Public showers
      • Food banks
      • Employment training centers
      • Employment offices
      • Goodwill, Salvation Army, and other similar organizations
      • Resource centers
      • Free clothing and home furnishings
      • Free childcare
      • Cash for Trash drop-offs
      • Emergency care and medical services
      • Social security offices
      • Free classes for learning basic skills like budgeting, English/language classes, etc.
      • Locations of free cell phone charging stations
  • A mobile/mobile-friendly web application that builds empathy between non-homeless and homeless individuals.
    LEARN MORE
    • Key Features:
      • Immersive experience (using augmented or virtual reality, AR/VR)
      • Token or award system to spur action
      • Offers real-life opportunities to meet (meet-ups, mentoring models, etc.)
  • An online, easy-to-apply-to matching program between homeless individuals seeking employment and local employers willing to hire (for specific job-related skills/traits).
    LEARN MORE
    • Key Features:
      • Location-based searches and suggestions
      • Accurate information about wages/salary (ideally, $22+/hr)
      • Simple, one-click application
      • Updated information about key skills and qualifications
      • Curated list of employers willing to specifically hire this target group
      • Searchable by these key features (like wages, key skills/quals)

Due to the nature of the issue area we are addressing, these following design considerations are crucial for a competitive submission:

  • Simplicity of application / usability for end users
  • Mobile-optimized Web application preferred
  • Easily integrated with City and County data management systems (HMIS) when applicable
  • Data protection and privacy

The Prize: Three finalists will receive $5K each, with the winner to receive an additional $5K, in funding for prototyping their submissions.
