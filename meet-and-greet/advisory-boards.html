Advisory Boards

We are fortunate enough to live in one of the most innovative regions in the world. We have leveraged this innovative region through strategic advisory boards to provide insights on a range of topics related to technology and innovation. By gathering thought leaders and cutting-edge innovators, we are ensuring we are building a sustainable and long-lasting vision for a Smart San José.

Smart Cities Advisory Board

Quarterly (Active)

Forward thinking advice to transform San José into a Smart City

The role of the Smart City Advisory board is to provide technology & creative advice and assistance in the implementation of Smart City Projects for the City of San José. These projects, falling under the purview of Deputy City Manager Kip Harkness, benefit from the expertise of industry leaders in related industries, including, but not limited to: Internet of Things (IoT), software, and Internet/connectivity.

Mayor's Innovation Circle

Bi-Annually (Active)

Keeping the pulse on the Valley's best ideas

Mayor Sam Liccardo’s Office in San José in partnership with the Silicon Valley Leadership Group has created Mayor's Innovation Circle. This select group of senior academic, non-profit and business leaders will provide insights around technology and innovation to inform the Mayor’s Smart City Vision to make San José the most innovative city in America by 2020.

Cybersecurity Advisory Board

Quarterly (upcoming)

Helping guide the online security of our City

The role of the Cyber Security Advisory Board will be to counsel and advise the City of San José’s cybersecurity infrastructure and practices. The board will work closely with Dr. Marcelo Peredo, Chief Information Security Officer for the City of San José.

Information Technology (IT) Advisory Board

Quarterly (completed)

Building data-centrism into our DNA

The role of the information technology advisory board was to provide expert direction in key strategic decisions for the City of San José’s Information Technology, especially as they related to being “brilliant at the basics” and building a city with data-centrism at its core, as well as investing in IT given the constrained resources of a city.
