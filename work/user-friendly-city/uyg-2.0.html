Unleash Your Geek 2.0: Low-Cost Sensors to Combat Flooding

Working with local universities to prepare for natural disasters
USER-FRIENDLY CITY

What's the problem we're solving?

The sudden and destructive flooding that occurred in February 2017 was the worst San José has seen in decades, resulting in the emergency evacuation of approximately 14,000 residents overnight. As extreme weather incidences make flooding more likely, San José needs a more responsive and accurate flood detection system to ensure residents are evacuated in a timely and safe manner. The call to action for Unleash Your Geek 2.0 arose in response to needs that were identified by the City and Water District following the destructive flooding and slow emergency response to it. To address these issues, student teams from local universities are collaborating directly with emergency responders and other key stakeholders within the city to design and prototype a flood sensing system that allows for quicker communication between the Water District, relevant City departments, and residents.

What's our srategy?

A joint effort between Santa Clara University’s Frugal Innovation Hub and San José State University’s Robotics, Sensors, and Machine Intelligence Lab is helping to address this challenge. The joint student teams are collaborating closely with the City and Water District to create a smart flood detection sensor that not only measures water height more accurately than the more expensive sensors currently deployed on City bridges, but which is AI-enabled to capture and classify images of water and debris-flows to build a better, more encompassing flood-monitoring system. It is an experiment, but one we hope will improve our ability to use data to keep residents safer

Our Impact

The 2017 San José floods led to loss of property, displaced residents, and negative impacts for many. An early warning flood monitoring system will not only prevent and mitigate future loss, but allow for the City and Water District to gain key insights about its waterways and natural landscapes through the image-capture and analytical functions of their partnership with the SCU-SJSU student teams

Thank you to the Frugal Innovation Hub at Santa Clara University’s School of Engineering and San José State University for your ongoing efforts to develop and deploy a smart flood detection sensor to prevent destructive flooding in San José.
