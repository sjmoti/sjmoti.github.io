Unleash Your Geek 1.0: Cleaning graffiti with drones

Calling upon the brilliant technical minds in San José
USER-FRIENDLY CITY

In May of 2016, the Mayor launched Unleash Your Geek 1.0 to challenge residents to create a technology solution to make graffiti abatement more efficient.

Currently, removing graffiti from overpasses, freeway signs, sound walls and railroad trestles is labor-intensive and expensive. A recent effort to clean graffiti along Highway 101 required closing freeways lanes for three nights, the mobilization of large bucket cranes and other equipment, and dozens of city and state personnel – ultimately costing up to $60,000 each time.

In addition to the huge expense, the complexity of coordinating such an effort often means that the graffiti will remain there for weeks or months before it can be removed. Results have so far been short-lived, with graffiti appearing rapidly after the city repaints the tagged areas.

This first Unleash Your Geek (UYG) challenge asked the community to develop a rapidly deployable solution that can dramatically reduce the cost of graffiti cleanups.

We received 140 submissions, with ideas going from drones, wall-crawling robots, chemical solutions, and more. From these submissions, we selected four finalists that were each given $5,000 by Microsoft and work space at Prospect Silicon Valley to turn their vision into a working prototype.

In September 2017, we selected a winner: San José residents Candace Marbury and Chris Farmer successfully demoed a graffiti-removing drone on stage at the 3rd Annual Innovate Your State Civic and Gov Tech Showcase.

Thank you to Chris Farmer, winner of Unleash Your Geek 1.0, Caltrans, and San José’s Department of Transportation for championing the implementation of the GRAD drone. Thank you also to our partners: USPTO, Prospect Silicon Valley, and Microsoft.
