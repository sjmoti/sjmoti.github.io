My San José app

Making service requests a snap
USER-FRIENDLY CITY

One of our dreams is for city services to to be as easy as ordering packing tape from Amazon Prime. We’re a ways away from getting there, but the My San José app is a significant step in the right direction. As smartphones become pervasive, we knew we needed an app for residents to easily ask the city to fix potholes or pick up a stray mattress from the sidewalk.

One of the decisions we made early on was to not take the easy route of simply building an email service where user requests get sent into an inbox. Instead, we engineered our service request process so that requests are directly routed into the workflow of service teams helping us to improve our service and speed response times.

Another decision was that we wanted to be as user-centric as possible in our design process. To that end, we worked with Code for San José to conduct usability tests with over 200 power users. We included features like tracking responses to services requests, following requests made by others in your neighborhood, and interactive maps of all requests in the city.

In 2018 alone, we’ve received over 110,000 service requests through the app. Our work is nowhere close to being done though. As with any good software product, we must continue to iterate and improve on the product with the help of our users.

Thanks to the My San José team in the city of San José for all of the hard work in innovating for public good!
