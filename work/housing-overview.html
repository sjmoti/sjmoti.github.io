Our Work Housing

Housing

With San José’s population set to grow 40% by 2040, our current housing pressures will only increase. Addressing housing issues is complex and requires responding to the crisis through multiple approaches on the local and regional level. However, we can alleviate some of the pressure with the smart use of data and technology.

Prioritizing housing inspections with big data

We worked with the University of Chicago to create algorithms to prioritize which multiple housing units our inspectors should visit first. This effort made us significantly better finding serious violations and will keep our population safer.

Renters' Rights App

Many San José residents rent, but oftentimes they don’t know their rights when facing challenges around renting properties. So, a volunteer group from Code for San José created a rental rights resource for our residents.

LEARN MORE

Building a rent registry that works for everyone

There are nearly 40,000 rent control units in San José, but it’s a challenge to ensure the rates abide by the city’s current ordinances. To address this need, we worked with IBM to design a user-centric rent registry that works for tenants and landlords alike to track the data.
