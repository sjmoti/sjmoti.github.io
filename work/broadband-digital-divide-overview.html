Our Work Broadband & Digital Divide

Broadband & Digital Divide

Today, access to the internet is essential for everything from doing homework to applying for jobs. Unfortunately 95,000 residents in San José don’t have access to internet at home, and many of them are children.
We are committed to closing this digital divide. Through innovative partnerships with the private sector, San José aims to both upgrade the city’s broadband infrastructure for all residents and close the digital divide for the city’s most vulnerable. We also aim to create a business-friendly environment to spur innovation around broadband to make it cheaper to deploy and more accessible to everyone. Our team is nationally recognized as a leader in this space both in implementation and in policy. In November 2017, our Chief Innovation Officer testified before the U.S. Congress on these issues

5g small cell network

In 2018, City Council unanimously approved deals with three large telecoms to deploy 4,000+ 5G small cells – the largest such deployment in the country. Proceeds from this deal will be used to create a $20M+ Digital Inclusion Fund and improve the broadband deployment process. These deals are used as a national model by FCC Commissioner Rosenworcel, the U.S. Conference of Mayors, and the National League of Cities.

Closing the digital divide

Today, we have over 95,000 residents without broadband internet access. On May 1, 2018, the San José City Council approved the allocation of funds raised from new small cell agreements to be allocated to a “Digital Inclusion Fund” to close the digital divide for our most vulnerable residents —- especially low-income children who can’t do their homework, the elderly, and the disabled. This hidden issue keeps those unconnected locked out of educational and employment opportunities.
