Solving the Urban Bandwidth Challenge

Terragraph is a 60GHz multi-hop multi-point wireless distribution network that delivers gigabit speeds to urban and suburban areas. The technology utilizes commercial off-the-shelf components and is optimized for high volume, low-cost production and ease of installation.

Advantages of the Terragraph technology:

  • Uses small nodes that are designed to be mounted on street furniture and buildings
  • Leverages the cloud for intensive data processing and self-organization
  • Extends fiber wirelessly throughout a city and delivers Gbps speeds

Facebook has installed over 250 Terragraph nodes on infrastructure in areas of the city's downtown corridor. The city is making sure that the nodes are attached safely and don’t disrupt traffic and light pole function.

The partnership with Facebook is an example of a San José that’s open for business – we want to be a testing ground for new, cutting-edge technology that can improve the lives of our residents.

This innovative “demonstration project” allows the city to innovate and learn how to efficiently deploy technology on our infrastructure and ultimately lower the cost of deployment. For Facebook, it offers the opportunity to conduct testing and validation of Terragraph technology.

Check out this jointly authored paper with Facebook to advance lowering the cost of deploying broadband through these kind of solutions: Urban Network Densification. You can also see this website to learn more: Terragraph: Solving the Urban Bandwidth Challenge.
