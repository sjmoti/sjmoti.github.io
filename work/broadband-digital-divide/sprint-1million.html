Working with Sprint to connect low-income high schoolers

Helping San José students stay connected beyond the classroom
BROADBAND & DIGITAL DIVIDE

Today, a lot of homework is done with the help of the internet. For students who don’t have access to internet at home, they’re at a significant disadvantage. They have to go over to a friend’s house, write out essays on their phones, or even sit outside the library after dark to get their homework done.

To help address the problem, we worked with Sprint to give over 4,000 high school students internet-connected devices for all 4 years of high school. The devices give students three gigabytes of high speed data per month until graduation.

This effort is part of Sprint's 1Million Project, a national program with the goal of helping a million high school students who don’t have access to internet at home.

When speaking about the project, Mayor Liccardo said: “In Silicon Valley, we have shown over and over again that if given the right tools, our students can do extraordinary things. If we are going to continue to be that place, we have to provide students with the tools to start.”
