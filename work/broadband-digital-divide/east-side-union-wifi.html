Free Wifi for East Side Union High School District

Working with ESUHSD to provide wifi in some of San José’s poorest neighborhoods
BROADBAND & DIGITAL DIVIDE

The internet is becoming an increasingly essential part of getting school work done today. An FCC taskforce reported that 70% of teachers assigned homework requiring internet access. We’ve heard stories of students sitting outside the library or schools after dark just to get their homework done, sometimes with just a mobile phone. In a story about our effort to close the homework gap, The Mercury News captures why this is such an important issue:

To do schoolwork, “I ask my cousin if I can borrow her computer,” said senior Julissa Aguirre, 17, who has no Wi-Fi at home. To connect, Julissa turns her cellphone into a Wi-Fi “hot spot.” She tries to finish as much work as she can in class.

Having no computer can be a hardship. “It’s kind of hard to type an essay on your phone,” Julissa said.

For students, internet access is essential. “Most of our work is online. If you don’t have Wi-Fi at home, how are you going to finish it?” said Overfelt junior Fredic Delrio, 16. And though many Overfelt families can access the internet, their connections often aren’t speedy. “It’s a lot slower,” Fredic said.

Now, East Side Union High School district and San José are working together to provide free wifi to students in some of the city’s poorest areas.

The projected is funded by a $2.7 million bonds raised by East Side, and city crews will install, operate, and maintain the wifi infrastructure and extending San José’s city wifi. We hope that with projects like this one, students can play on an even playing field and ultimately have a fair shot to learn.
