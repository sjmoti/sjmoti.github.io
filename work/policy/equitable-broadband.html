Equitable Broadband Deployment

Fighting for equitable deployment of 5G internet and net neutrality
POLICY

In 2017, Mayor Liccardo was appointed to the FCC’s Broadband Deployment Advisory Committee, a national body of community and business leaders that recommends policies to the FCC around broadband. However, unfortunately the body was stacked with industry personnel with few local government voices advocating for the public. After working in good faith for over 9 months on the body and despite the best effort of many individuals, in January of 2018, Mayor Liccardo resigned from the committee, citing his frustration with the group’s clear bias toward serving industry interest. Read his resignation letter from the FCC's Broadband Deployment Advsiory Committee. We believe in embracing technology in San Jose, but we put the public interest first.

We didn’t walk away from this fight - in fact we raised our concerns in Congress and the Senate. To learn more, check out Mayor Liccardo's press release on his resignation from the FCC Committee as well as San José Chief Innovation Officer Shireen Santosham’s Reddit AMA on the topic or her Congressional testimony on rolling out 5G internet equitably.

Here at home in San Jose, we doubled down to make sure our residents received improved broadband service and took steps to address the digital divide. We recently signed deals to get over $500M in broadband investment into San Jose and set up a Digital Inclusion fund to bridge the digital divide for our most vulnerable residents.

Mayor Liccardo also played a critical leadership role in combating SB 649, a damaging state level broadband bill that didn’t serve the public interest. You can read more in his New York Times op-ed about SB 649. Under pressure of 300+ California cities and other partners, Governor Brown vetoed the bill last year.

Our office also support net neutrality on the state and national levels. In fact, Mayor Liccardo also recently hosted the introduction of a state net neutrality bill here at San José City Hall.

We will continue to raise the voice of our community at the state and national level to fight for broadband for all.
