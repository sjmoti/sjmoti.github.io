Our Work Policy California SB 649

Fighting California State Bill 649

Making sure the public voice is included in the deployment of 5G internet
POLICY

As Mayor Sam Liccardo wrote in his New York Times op-ed, “Public street poles may not look like much, but to wireless service providers, they’re valuable real estate.” Telecoms want to install wifi equipment on light poles, but cities are careful not to subsidize industry with taxpayer dollars by giving access at below-market rates.

Telecoms simultaneously introduced bills in 20 states that would preempt cities from being able to give input on how, when, and where small cells can be deployed. Essentially, the public would not have input if a refrigerator-sized device shows up on the light pole outside their homes. To be clear, we want next generation broadband to be deployed, we simply think that the public should have a voice.

There was enough political support in the senate for the bill, but San José took leadership and helped organize many of California’s cities to put our recommendation forward to Governor Brown. In the end, Governor Brown made the decision to veto the bill so a more balanced one could be drafted.
