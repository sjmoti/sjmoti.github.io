Shireen Santosham

Chief Innovation Officer

 @SSantosham

Shireen Santosham is a tech optimist. In 2016, under Mayor Liccardo, she launched San José’s Smart City Vision initiative and has since championed affordable broadband, autonomous vehicle pilots, a comprehensive privacy policy, and apps and social media help residents interact with the city more easily.

Prior to joining the city, she launched innovative initiatives across sectors. She led research to size the gender gap in internet access for women in the developing world while a Director at GSMA, helped to launch the Center for Government while at McKinsey & Company, helped to secure $50M to combat global overfishing while at Oceana, and helped to stand up the Allen Institute for Brain Science while an impact investor at Vulcan Capital.

She was named to GovTech’s List of 25 Doers, Dreamers, and Drivers in 2018, and her work has been featured in Bloomberg Technology, KQED, and Ars Technica. She has spoken at the Consumer Electronics Show, Mobile World Congress, the United Nations Beijing +20 conference, the United States Congress, Harvard University, UC Berkeley, Stanford University, and Columbia University.

Shireen holds an M.B.A. from Harvard Business School, an M.P.A in International Development from Harvard Kennedy School, and a B.S. and B.A. with honors from the University of Pennsylvania. She is a fellow at the Young Leaders Forum hosted by the National Committee on U.S.-China Relations and previously a term member at the Council on Foreign Relations and Global Governance Futures Fellow at the Bosch Foundation.

Denzil Sikka

Harvard Business School Leadership Fellow

 @denzils91


Denzil is a San José native with a passion for disrupting stagnant industries through the use of technology. She last worked as a software engineer at Yammer (secretly, a Microsoft company). Before that, Denzil tackled the roles of PM at PowerPoint and an adjunct lecturer of Computer Science at Foothill Community College and San Francisco State University. Her interests include crowdsourcing community initiatives, leveraging data to find innovative solutions to long-standing challenges, and creating solutions to help users increase productivity while building their digital footprint. In her spare time, she enjoys playing the piano and authoring op-eds and fiction while sipping green tea. Denzil holds a MBA from Harvard Business School and a BS & MS in computer science from MIT.

Vicki Sun

Technology & Innovation Advisor | Community Engagement Manager

 @vickisun


Vicki brings extensive experience in community engagement, fundraising, and education technology as a grant writer and program coordinator for the Los Angeles Promise Neighborhood initiative and other edtech-based community turnaround solutions as a Stanford Public Interest Network Fellow at a non-profit in Los Angeles. She has also served as a grant writer and program officer at the Asian Institute of Technology in Thailand, where she helped deploy professional development trainings on new technologies to enable the sustainable development of South Asian and ASEAN member countries. Vicki also helped launch a college preparation start-up to ensure more diverse students access to great universities. Vicki holds a BA with honors and an MA in international education policy and evaluation from Stanford University.

Carolyn (Qiaojie) Wu

Technology & Innovation Advisor | Smart City Manager

 @QiaojieWu


Carolyn focuses on the strategy and policy development around autonomous vehicle, IoT/broadband, data analytics and some demonstration initiatives. Previously, Carolyn was a management consultant in the Boston Consulting Group, where she had extensive project experience with governments and international organizations. During her study in Harvard Kennedy School, she wrote her thesis on the role of government in fostering Artificial Intelligence.

Carolyn holds an MPA in International Development from Harvard Kennedy School and BBA in Hong Kong University of Science and Technology.

David Albán Hidalgo

Technology & Innovation Advisor | SPIN Fellow

 @dalbanhi


David (pronounced dah-VEED) brings a range of technical experiences including software development, user-design/prototyping, and data management/visualization through an internship at Microsoft’s .NETCoreFX team and various academic projects. Some projects include managing/organizing hundreds of hours of oral history interviews of undocumented Mexican immigrant interviews, creating data bases/geographic visualizations of music-based migrant organizations in Buenos Aires, and helping design and prototype an app facilitating parent-teacher communication for preschool students.

David holds a BA with Honors, a BS, and an MA from Stanford University. Originally from Quito, Ecuador he speaks Spanish natively and Quechua and French at conversational levels.
